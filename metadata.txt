Engels:
This master thesis text is about the real-time adjustment of bicycle cadence applied to the E-bike of IntuEdrive. The text is the last of a series of 
works concerning the E-bike concept of Tomas Keppens: a fast and reliable E-bike that is easy to use. This thesis continues on the current prototype. 
The goal is to predict the pedaling speed of the cyclist and thus automatically changing the bicycle gear. This pedaling speed varies per user. The 
first step is creating a parameterized simulation, so that much consistent data is easily generated. Next, various machine learning algorithms, like 
Passive Aggressive algorithm, decision trees and random forest, are discussed and evaluated. Important performance indicators are the number of times 
needed to train the model, execution time and accuracy. It is checked whether the algorithms can handle a stochastic update strategy. People after all 
are unpredictable. Last but not least, the problem of conceptual drift is looked over. A bicycle can be used by several people, each with a different 
preferred pedaling speed. The results show that a random forest performs the best in various experiments.
